Optional Deep Dive: The Human Element of HVAC

For those who need to understand the full picture: how traditional systems impact human psychology and why true control is transformative.

Why trust collapsed: When occupants feel ignored by their environment, trust in the building management erodes. This isn't just about temperature; it's about perceived agency and respect. The "one-size-fits-all" approach of centralized HVAC actively fosters distrust.

What cortisol and override behavior tell you: Uncomfortable temperatures elevate stress hormones like cortisol, impairing focus and emotional regulation. Frequent manual overrides are a direct symptom of this psychological distress, leading to a vicious cycle where attempts to gain control inadvertently increase energy waste and system wear.

Why adaptive comfort theory changed the codes: Research shows people tolerate a wider range of temperatures when they have control. This "adaptive comfort" principle is now recognized in standards like ASHRARE 55. Empowering occupants with control not only boosts comfort but also allows for broader temperature setpoints, leading to significant energy savings without sacrificing well-being. SentientZone leverages this by giving control back to the individual, aligning human psychology with energy efficiency goals.
